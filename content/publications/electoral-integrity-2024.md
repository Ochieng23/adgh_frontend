## Executive Summary

This assessment examines the state of electoral integrity across the fifteen member states of the Economic Community of West African States (ECOWAS) during the 2024 electoral cycle. Drawing on the African Charter on Democracy, Elections and Governance (ACDEG) framework, we evaluate five core dimensions: legal framework quality, electoral management body independence, campaign finance transparency, voter participation rates, and post-election dispute resolution mechanisms.

## Key Findings

### Legal Framework Quality

The majority of ECOWAS member states have enacted constitutional provisions and electoral laws broadly consistent with ACDEG principles. However, significant implementation gaps persist, particularly in areas relating to:

- Timely legal reforms ahead of electoral cycles
- Consistency between constitutional provisions and subsidiary electoral legislation
- Judicial independence in electoral dispute adjudication

### Electoral Management Body Independence

Electoral management bodies (EMBs) remain the single most critical institutional variable in determining electoral integrity outcomes. Our assessment found that EMBs with constitutionally guaranteed independence, adequate resources, and non-partisan appointment processes consistently delivered higher-quality electoral processes.

**Recommended Actions:**
- Constitutional entrenchment of EMB independence across all member states
- Regional technical assistance framework for EMB capacity building
- Peer review mechanism under ECOWAS auspices

### Voter Participation and Inclusion

Voter turnout across the region ranged from 38% to 74%, with significant variations driven by:

1. Urban/rural access disparities
2. Youth disillusionment with political processes
3. Women's participation barriers including security concerns and social norms
4. Voter registration system quality

## Recommendations

The ADGH calls upon ECOWAS member states and the Commission to:

1. **Strengthen the ECOWAS Democracy and Good Governance Protocol** with binding implementation timelines and accountability mechanisms
2. **Establish a Regional Electoral Integrity Fund** to support EMB capacity building
3. **Adopt a gender-responsive electoral framework** mandating minimum thresholds for women's political participation
4. **Invest in civic education programmes** to address declining youth voter turnout
5. **Develop a regional early warning system** for electoral violence prevention

## Conclusion

West Africa's democratic governance trajectory remains one of the most dynamic on the continent. While challenges persist, the collective commitment of ECOWAS member states to the democratic governance framework provides a strong foundation for continued progress. This assessment calls for renewed commitment to the ACDEG principles and accelerated implementation of regional governance standards.
