## Overview

Africa's demographic dividend presents a historic opportunity for democratic governance transformation. With over 60% of the African population under 25 years of age, the active political engagement of young Africans is not merely desirable — it is indispensable to the continent's democratic future.

Yet structural barriers continue to exclude young people from meaningful political participation: age restrictions on candidacy, resource barriers to political competition, party gatekeeping, and persistent narratives that frame youth as political spectators rather than agents.

## Key Barriers Identified

1. **Legal and constitutional barriers** — Age thresholds for candidacy that effectively exclude most young Africans
2. **Financial barriers** — Campaign finance systems that favour incumbent elites
3. **Institutional barriers** — Party structures that marginalise youth wings
4. **Cultural barriers** — Gerontocratic norms that delegitimise youth political authority

## Policy Recommendations

Governments should reduce candidacy age thresholds, introduce youth quotas in legislative chambers, and provide public campaign finance accessible to first-time candidates. Civil society organisations should invest in youth political leadership development. Regional bodies should incorporate youth participation benchmarks into their democratic governance review frameworks.
