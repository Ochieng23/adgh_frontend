**ADDIS ABABA, 1 October 2024** — The African Democracy and Governance Hub (ADGH) today announced the official launch of its continental governance monitoring initiative, a landmark platform designed to systematically track, assess, and report on democratic governance trends across all 55 African Union member states.

The initiative represents a major step in ADGH's mission to provide evidence-based advocacy and research in support of democratic governance across Africa, fully aligned with the African Charter on Democracy, Elections and Governance (ACDEG) and the African Union's Agenda 2063.

## About the Initiative

The ADGH Governance Monitoring Platform will:

- **Track ACDEG implementation** across all ratifying and non-ratifying states
- **Monitor electoral processes** through a network of accredited civil society partners
- **Assess institutional governance quality** using an Africa-specific methodology developed in partnership with leading African universities
- **Publish annual country governance profiles** available openly to citizens, governments, and researchers

## Leadership Statement

Speaking at the launch ceremony in Addis Ababa, ADGH Chief Executive Gabriella Lorere said:

> "This platform is built on a simple but powerful idea: that African citizens deserve accurate, timely, and independent information about the quality of governance in their countries. Information is the foundation of accountability, and accountability is the foundation of democracy."

## Next Steps

The first annual governance monitoring report will be published in the first quarter of 2025, covering all 55 AU member states. Civil society organisations wishing to participate as monitoring partners should contact partnerships@adgh.org.
